# SNOW OVER LIAODONG — Super-Detailed Act Breakdown
### The campaign exactly as currently built and deployed (post PASS-2)

> This documents what **exists in the live build right now** — ten missions across three acts, before the new Act 2/3 expansion (Namgil, the Joseon-court mission, the network-fracture) that was just proposed and not yet built. Where the just-approved plan will change something, it is noted as **[PLANNED]** so you can see current-vs-future at a glance.

## MISSION 1 — "The Archer's Daughter"
**Scene IDs:** ch1 → a1_drill → a1_raidgo → [CINE: ford] → a1_rescue → ch2

**Location:** Uiju, Joseon — a border garrison village above the frozen Yalu River. Deep winter. Seol is 11.

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch1 (chapter card):** Establishes her — "You are Baek Seol — 백설, white snow. Your father is an archer of the border garrison, and you are all he has." He teaches her the bow at dawn and bare-handed ssireum at dusk "until the boys refuse to face you."
- **a1_drill (father speaks):** The dawn lesson. The line the whole game is built on: *"A blade in front of you is a problem. A blade behind you is a funeral. Never stop listening behind you."* He gives her both the far fight (bow) and the near one (fists).
- **a1_raidgo:** Smoke on the north wind. Jurchen raiders cross the frozen river into the millet. Her father runs toward her — and doesn't reach her in time. Rope. A saddle. The village burning.
- **[FORD CUTSCENE — 16s staged]:** Camera orbits slowly downward to the river's edge. He catches the raiders alone — one man against seven — kills six (the first with an arrow, the last with his hands), reaches her, and in the embrace his arms go slack: the seventh man, from behind. *"He was holding you when he stopped listening."*
- **a1_rescue (father):** The scene resolution of the same beat — "you feel it before you understand it."

**Gameplay identity:** Tutorial. Movement, camera, the bow (X untrained), fists, first wave fight against raiders. Teaches the game's core verbs in a village under attack.

**Story purpose:** Establish who made Seol and take him away in fifteen minutes — so every later skill and every later father-figure is measured against this loss.

**Emotional purpose:** Inherited grief and inherited competence in one scene. Her father's love *is* his training.

**Skills:** Bow + Fists — present from the first minute, her father's whole inheritance.

**Connection to ending:** The finale returns to this exact ford. Her father "fought seven men" here; the last mission's subtitle says "You are what he made, finished by three more hands." The Winter Bow's first arrow is laid on his grave in the epilogue.

## MISSION 2 — "The Market of Old Kang"
**Scene IDs:** ch2 → a2_break → a2_close1(gameplay) → a2_kang → a2_zhao → ch3
**Chapter code:** MARKET

**Location:** Guangning, Ming Liaodong. A slave market between the silk and salt-fish stalls. Weeks after the raid.

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch2:** "They sell people here between the silk and the salt fish. The fat merchant who owns the chains is called Kang. He smiles at buyers and counts everything." Her wrists have learned his ropes; today the knot is careless.
- **a2_break:** She slips the rope, drops a handler with his own club, and runs — *not for the gate, for the cages.* (Her defining reflex, established the first time she's free.)
- **[gameplay]:** Fight Kang's handlers with bow/fists; free three cages of captives.
- **a2_kang (Kang speaks):** The fat merchant appears, rings on every finger, and backhands her to the stones: *"Do you know what you have cost me?"*
- **a2_zhao (Zhao speaks):** An officer's boots between her and the second blow. General Zhao. *"Then you won't mind selling it cheap."* He looks at her "the way a man looks at a blade he has found in a scrap heap." *"I watched her fight. You had six men. She was winning."* He buys her.

**Gameplay identity:** First real combat sandbox — handlers, cage-freeing as quick-gates.

**Story purpose:** Introduce the two men who define her captivity (Kang, the trafficker with a merchant's mask) and her rescue (Zhao, who values her for competence, not pity).

**Emotional purpose:** Seol at her most feral; establishes that her instinct is always *toward the cages.* Zhao's respect precedes any affection — she is bought like a blade, which is morally uncomfortable in exactly the intended way.

**Political purpose:** Establishes the machinery of the era — human beings sold openly in a Ming city; a general who moves through it with authority.

**Connection to ending:** Kang returns as the thematic antagonist (exploitation); "You had six men. She was winning" is echoed in Zhao's final farewell ("I watched you fight six men with your hands tied wrong").

## MISSION 3 — "Five Winters" (The House of Zhao)
**Scene IDs:** ch3 → a3_go → a3_y2 → a3_y3 → a3_y4 → [CINE: kiss] → a3_dance → [CINE: mingzhu] → a3_fall → a3_window → a3_vow → ch4
**Chapter code:** ESTATE

**Location:** The Zhao garrison estate, Liaodong. Ages 11 → 16, one gate per year.

**Beat-by-beat (the emotional heart of the whole game):**
- **ch3:** "You arrive at eleven, feral and silent. You leave at sixteen changed."
- **a3_go — YEAR ONE (Mingzhu):** The general's wife doesn't coo — she puts a brush in Seol's hand. *"Characters first. Then manners. Then everything else."* When a visiting magistrate's wife calls Seol "that Korean thing," Mingzhu sets down her cup: *"In this house, we name people or we starve. Which will you choose?"* **THE SEED:** walking the hills, she nods at a dark seam in the rock — *"I love that cave. For the freedom it allows."* Seol, 11, files it under things grown women say. That night Seol thanks her, unprompted, in Chinese. "Her whole face changes."
- **a3_y2 — YEAR TWO (Zhao):** He sees footwork in how she beats dust from a quilt. A wooden sword appears by her mat. *"Your father gave you a soldier's knife; the general means to give you a swordsman's forms."* **SWORD + TRAINED KIT UNLOCK.** "Praise is a nod. You would kill for the nod."
- **a3_y3 — YEAR THREE (Lan + Jin):** **Lan**, four years older, drills her in the counter: *"Strength borrows. Balance owns."* **COUNTER-THROW (skill ③) UNLOCKS.** And **Jin** — the son who "dances," whom Zhao dismisses ("My son dances"). Seol spars him to humiliate him and loses: "He was never where your blade was — like fencing smoke that smiles." *"It's just listening — with your whole body."*
- **a3_y4 — YEAR FOUR (the plot engine):** Zhao asks about her family in rough Korean. She names **Kang as the raiders' master and an intelligence-seller to the Jurchen.** *"I thought he traded slaves. He trades in my country's throat."* Zhao begins his second ledger.
- **[KISS CUTSCENE — 12s]:** The pond, evening. "Stop trying to beat me. Move with me." The sword-dance becomes one turning shape; the camera drifts up to the lanterns and snow at the moment of contact. *"The kiss tastes like the beginning of something. It is actually the end of everything."*
- **[MINGZHU DEATH CUTSCENE — 18s]:** Camera descends over the estate for 14 seconds, then holds *perfectly still* for the last four while snow falls. Fushun falls, then Tieling; a strike on the eastern estate "guided by someone who knew the roads"; Mingzhu killed at the granary. *"The general does not weep. Lan does not weep. Jin goes still — and stays still. China is falling. In this house, it began with her."*
- **a3_fall (aftermath):** The funeral. Zhao "holding a wall up with his back," Lan's shaking hands over her mother's accounts. Seol reaches for the old anger and it won't come: *"you had a mother again, and you knew it only long enough to lose her."* The planted phrase returns: *"Guided by someone who knew the roads. You will remember that phrase."*
- **a3_window:** Jin grieves in "motion arrested — the dancer gone stone." He leaves at a grey dawn. From a window Seol watches him go; at the gate he looks up, finds her window without searching, holds her eyes three heartbeats. *"He never said goodbye to you. Perhaps that was the goodbye."*
- **a3_vow (Zhao):** *"Will you serve China? Or go home, to your people?"* — *"You are my people too. I'll fight with you. But when we find Koreans in chains, we free them. Every one. That is my price."* Zhao — who nods instead of praising — crosses the room and holds her like a daughter, and she feels her father's arms at the ford five years gone.

**Gameplay identity:** Estate exploration; training dummies; the mid-mission sword unlock permanently changes the combat kit.

**Story purpose:** Build the family the war will break. Each year = one relationship = one skill or one plot turn.

**Emotional purpose:** She gains and loses a second mother; gains a love and loses him; gains a father through grief. The densest emotional mission in the game.

**Skills:** Sword + Dash (Zhao, Y2), Counter-Throw (Lan, Y3). Jin's dance is *taught* here but unlocks mechanically later.

**Connection to ending:** Every teacher-skill pays off in the finale ("trained by an archer of Joseon, by a general of Ming, by a dancer"). The cave seed germinates in Mission 5. The vow is Seol's entire mission statement.

## MISSION 4 — "Two Masks"
**Scene IDs:** ch4 → a4_go → a4_clash → a4_team → a4_close → a4_ambushcut → [WAVE + CINE: recognition] → a4_recognize → a4_leftme → a4_hadit → ch5
**Chapter code:** MASKED

**Location:** Rooftops of a fallen Liaodong town, deep night. Years later — Seol is now an adult agent of Zhao's covert network.

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch4:** "You run missions for Zhao's network now — a quiet war of ledgers, safehouses, and opened cages. You work alone. You prefer it."
- **a4_clash (Jin, masked):** Two masked crews converge on the same rescue. A tall masked stranger: "This compound is spoken for." — "Funny. I was about to say that."
- **a4_team:** His way is misdirection (no one touched); hers is a knife through the dark. They split the compound out of spite.
- **a4_ambushcut:** The last cage opens on empty straw — a trap. The stranger draws with a dancer's roll of the wrist: "Do try to keep up."
- **[RECOGNITION CUTSCENE — 10s]:** Accelerating camera orbit. Overwhelmed, Seol drops into the dance without thinking — and the stranger flows into it with her "like he has been waiting years for the invitation." *"You know this dance. You know this dancer."* **THE DANCE ULT (④) UNLOCKS at this exact beat.**
- **a4_recognize:** "...Jin?"
- **a4_leftme:** *"You left us. You left me."* He doesn't defend himself — his eyes go past her shoulder and he dashes, hand at her waist, blade over her shoulder into a soldier rising behind her. (The father's death inverted: an embrace, danger from behind — but this time the blade doesn't land, because someone who reads the room is holding her.) "For half a heartbeat you are sixteen, by a pond, in lantern light."
- **a4_hadit:** *"I had it."* — then, unlevel — *"And don't touch me. I'm mad at you."* He steps back: "I know you don't need me to save you. You never did." He leaves a clumsy Korean riddle — *"Where the water is quiet like a mirror… where the mountain sees itself. When the sun is highest. Tomorrow."* — and is gone. *"He speaks Korean. When did he —"*

**Gameplay identity:** Full trained combat; patrol guards; the trap wave; the ult unlock as the mechanical payoff of a story beat.

**Story purpose:** The reunion — recognition through *movement*, not faces. Establishes the adult relationship's wound (he left) and its future terms.

**Emotional purpose:** Seol's pride and her buried hurt in one beat ("I had it… don't touch me"); the deeper truth beneath both — you don't let yourself be held by someone who abandoned you. Jin's first act of growth: he doesn't follow.

**Skills:** The Dance ult (④).

**Connection to ending:** The dance-that-saves-lives becomes the thing that beats the un-chargeable Ohan ("you fight like four people who loved you"). The Korean he learned pays off across the whole back half.

## MISSION 5 — "The Lake & The Cave"
**Scene IDs:** ch5 → a5_go → a5_msg → [CINE: lake] → a5_lake → a5_cavego → [cave: fragments] → a5_stone → a5_letters → a5_third → [cave: weapons] → a5_sword → a5_bow → a5_intel → ch6
**Chapter code:** LAKE

**Location:** A mountain lake at noon; then Jin's secret cave hideout above the valley.

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch5 / a5_go / a5_msg:** The avoidance montage — she's "very busy, actually," sharpening an already-sharp blade, repacking, muttering ("He left ME." / "A riddle. A RIDDLE. Like I'm twelve."). The stand-by message removes her last excuse. *"…Aaaargh."*
- **[LAKE CUTSCENE — 12s]:** Slow push across the mirror-water toward the shore where Jin waits, unmoving — "as if stillness were the gift he prepared." "You came," in Korean, "still clumsy, still learned for you."
- **a5_lake:** "I had nothing better to do," she says, and sits anyway. The golden-hour talk — "Neither of you apologizes exactly. Both of you forgive approximately." Then: "There's somewhere I want to show you."
- **a5_cavego:** The cave — his hidden inner life: lanterns, hanging silk paintings, a black-and-gold spring, moss, a shaft of light. His history: echo (with friends) → singing (alone) → drawing (age 8, away from his parents). *"No one ever knew." — "Mingzhu did,"* Seol says, and repeats her Year-One line about the cave and freedom. Jin stops breathing. "...She what?"
- **[cave phase 1 — fragments]:** Find five wall-fragments, vertical in the cracks, written where lantern light doesn't fall.
- **a5_stone:** Assembled, Mingzhu's hidden message: she followed her son at eight, said nothing ("a named freedom dies"), came alone to write "in the dark, where no one reads… because from these walls I watched my children become themselves." *"Look beneath the singing stone."*
- **a5_letters:** The lacquered box — her own paintings under her *maiden-name* seal (she was a secret artist; it's where Jin comes from and he never knew). Three letters. **Jin's:** *"You will leave, my dove… go back for the girl. I watched you two by the pond — two rivers pretending they don't share a sea… be together; love loudly, for both of us."* — She predicted the window, forgave him in advance, and so, finally, does Seol: *"We're alright, Jin. We were always going to be."*
- **a5_third (Seol's letter):** *"For the daughter winter brought me… My father was a master blacksmith… Two of his works I kept… Take the high ledge and the water's secret."*
- **[cave phase 2 — weapons]:** Hunt the two hidden weapons.
- **a5_sword:** **The Crane's Wing** — an absurd reverse-curved sword only Jin could love, etched *"Dance, do not brawl; end, do not hate."* Flirtation: "It's hideous." / "It's *perfect*." (showing off for her.)
- **a5_bow:** **The Winter Bow** — the finest bow ever made, etched in *Korean* (learned just for Seol): *"Snow may fall; the arrow flies home."* She cries, then laughs, "and the acoustics of the cave forgive everything." **WINTER BOW UPGRADE: bow damage 15→22.**
- **a5_intel (Jin):** Kang moves a column of Koreans across the Yalu tonight; his escort is a raider captain named **Batu.** The name goes through her "like river cold." "Fight with me. As equals, or not at all." / "As equals. Try to keep up."

**Gameplay identity:** The most varied mission — comic gates, traversal, cutscene, a search/verticality phase, a weapon-hunt phase, a real weapon upgrade.

**Story purpose:** Mingzhu becomes the secret heart of the whole story; the romance is repaired *by the dead mother's letter*, not an apology.

**Emotional purpose:** Full repair of the romance; a mother arming her daughter across death; the game's quietest, warmest mission (the only warm-lit level in a game of snow and ice).

**Skills:** The Winter Bow (Mingzhu, posthumous).

**Connection to ending:** "The arrows fly home the way a mother promised they would" is the finale's line. The forgiveness here is why Jin can stand *beside* her at the end.

## MISSION 6 — "The Third Water"
**Scene IDs:** ch6 → a6_go → a6_batu → [WAVE boss] → a6_win → a6_free → a6_end → ch7
**Chapter code:** RIVER

**Location:** The frozen Yalu at night — the border itself, home visible on the far bank.

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch6:** Kang's column on the ice — chained captives, sledges of silver, riders in wolf-fur. Home "close enough to smell the woodsmoke." "The captives are the mission. Batu is yours."
- **a6_batu (Batu):** Older now, grey in the wolf-fur. *"The archer's girl. He was good, your father. Six of mine. I still count it." — "Count this,"* and she draws.
- **[BATU BOSS — the Charger]:** He commits to telegraphed straight-line charges; step aside, punish the vulnerable slide. "Batu commits to a line, the way linear men do. You are never on it." The ice takes him "with a single black swallow."
- **a6_win:** Kang captured, ledgers burning; Jin's dancers strike the chains.
- **a6_free (Seol) — the theme's first statement:** "The far bank is Joseon. Walk. Don't run — the ice holds if you walk." A girl hesitates — "what they'll say, about where I've been." *"Say it first, and say it loud. You survived. That is the whole story. Anyone ashamed of you should try surviving it themselves."* The girl gives her name: **Danbi, from the mill village below Uiju** — "offered like a debt acknowledged."
- **a6_end:** On the ice between two countries. "First — we go back to work. There are more cages." She turns from home "because turning from it is how you'll earn it." **END OF ACT ONE.**

**Gameplay identity:** Chain-striking quick-gates, escort combat, the Batu boss (charge mechanic).

**Story purpose:** Close the father's death (Batu, the seventh man); the first great liberation; the renunciation that ends Act One.

**Emotional purpose:** She avenges without being consumed; the hwanhyangnyeo theme is stated for the first time; Danbi planted for her finale payoff.

**Connection to ending:** Batu (charge) is the mechanical *thesis* the finale's Ohan (guard) inverts; Danbi returns in the last mission to say Seol's words back to a whole village.

## MISSION 7 — "The City That Held a Country"
**Scene IDs:** ch7 → a7_go → a7_lan → [WAVE] → a7_zhao → a7_dance → ch8
**Chapter code:** SIEGE

**Location:** Liaoyang, the Ming eastern capital, burning. Night. (Historical: the 1621 fall, with the real detail of traitors opening the sluice gates.)

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch7:** "Liaoyang — the great eastern capital — is burning from the inside. Traitors opened the sluice gates; the Eight Banners are in the streets. Zhao is in that city. Go get your father."
- **a7_lan (Lan) — [PASS-1 expanded]:** Lan holds a barricade "with the calm of a woman balancing accounts." Her motivation: the counter-ledger — *"Kang counts what he takes. I count what we take back… mine will be longer than his."* Her mother's letter: *"I'm starting with a barricade."* **[PASS-2 Ohan seed]:** she warns of an officer "who walks the captive pens with a brush and paper and counts… They call him the Wolf. A man who counts people. This one has an army."
- **[WAVE]:** Cut a path to the keep.
- **a7_zhao (Zhao):** The general in an empty command hall, writing "the last dispatch no one will ever read." *"I held this line eleven years. A man should die where he stood."* — *"A man should live where he's loved… Choose the war you can still win."* — *"…Mingzhu used to argue like that. Carry me out of my grave."*
- **a7_dance (Zhao) — the father-son payoff:** Above the burning streets, Jin's dancers evacuate civilians roof to roof, children passed hand to hand over the fire. Zhao: *"I called it dancing. All those years. I said it like a small word." — "It is dancing." — "Yes. It is the biggest word I know now."*

**Gameplay identity:** Burning-city combat; wave to the keep; the rooftop dance-rescue as spectacle.

**Story purpose (current):** Save Zhao; reconcile father and son; the war goes full-scale.

**Political purpose (current):** Zhao refuses the historical defector's path (Li Yongfang, who knelt at Fushun "and they made him rich").

**[PLANNED CHANGE]:** In the rebuild this material largely moves to the new Mission 9 ("The Machine, Not the Man"), and Mission 7 becomes **"The Name They Gave You"** — the refugee-camp defense that introduces the network's scale, the first fatal order, and Namgil (the internal antagonist). The Zhao/Jin rooftop beat is *preserved* but relocated.

## MISSION 8 — "The Ledger Road"
**Scene IDs:** ch8 → a8_go → a8_convoy → [WAVE] → a8_ledger → a8_jin → ch9
**Chapter code:** LEDGER

**Location:** A mountain pass in the western hills, midnight. Weeks after Liaoyang.

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch8:** Kang slipped his chains in Liaoyang's chaos and sold his masterwork — **the Great Ledger** (every route, safehouse, and name) — to a Banner officer called the Wolf. A copy travels the pass tonight. "It must not arrive."
- **a8_convoy:** A strongbox on a cart, wolf-fur riders, and captives roped behind. Jin: "Box or people first?" — "People. Always people. Then we burn his paperwork."
- **[WAVE]:** Take the convoy.
- **a8_ledger — [PASS-2 Ohan seed]:** The strongbox holds the Ledger and a sealed letter of sale: *"To Ohan of the Plain Blue Banner — the whole apparatus, for services rendered."* The margins are annotated in immaculate red ink — corrections, cross-references — and one entry circled twice: *"The girl. Fights like two schools. Confirm."* Jin: *"Kang sells. This one studies."*
- **a8_jin (Jin) — [PASS-1 added] the romance's adult milestone:** Night watch. *"Shenyang frightens me… All my life I've moved around things. Grief. My father. Walls… There's no around, where we're going. There's only through. That's your art, not mine."* He asks **her** to lead the Shenyang operation — *"You cut lines. I draw circles. Shenyang needs a line"* — the structural inverse of the waist-grab. Then, half-asleep, he practices Korean for her village: *"If I'm going to meet your village, I should greet them properly." You do not tell him his accent is terrible. You memorize it instead.*

**Gameplay identity:** Terraced night traversal; convoy ambush wave.

**Story purpose (current):** Establish the Great Ledger stakes; introduce Ohan by name and title; Jin's growth scene.

**[PLANNED CHANGE]:** In the rebuild this becomes **"Two Kings, One Girl"** — the keystone new mission: the Joseon court and Ming remnants both try to *own* Seol; she discovers her own homeland is ashamed of the returned women; she refuses both masters. Social-infiltration/stealth verb. The Ledger material folds into the new Mission 9.

## MISSION 9 — "The Belly of the Conquest"
**Scene IDs:** ch9 → a9_go → [cage-row quick-gates] → a9_kang → a9_out → ch10
**Chapter code:** YARDS

**Location:** The holding yards of Shenyang, the Qing capital. Night.

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch9:** "The yards of Shenyang: cage rows under torchlight, a bureaucracy of grief, clerks weighing people like rice. Every cage. Then Kang. In that order."
- **[three cage-row rescues]:** an old man's granddaughter; children going over the wall in the dancers' ribbons, "laughing — laughing — at the ride."
- **a9_kang (Kang) — the thematic climax:** He doesn't run — "Men like Kang never run; they renegotiate." *"Name a price. Everyone has one. I have met everyone."* — *"You met me twice. The day you sold me, and tonight. Both times you counted wrong."* The freed fill the doorway behind her. *"I'm not going to kill you… They get to. You're going home with them — roped to the last cart, past every village you emptied. Let them do the counting."* (Restorative justice, not vengeance — the theme applied to the villain.)
- **a9_out (Zhao) — [PASS-2 Ohan seed + the farewell]:** As the freed clear the wall, Ohan watches from the rampart — doesn't chase, *writes*, then inclines his head across the distance: "A debt, acknowledged, to be collected." Jin: "Ohan." Then **the farewell:** Zhao goes west with Lan to seed the wider resistance. *"You were never my servant, Seol. You were my second daughter from the day I watched you fight six men with your hands tied wrong." — "They were tied fine. I loosened them myself." — "I know. Go home, daughter. Walk tall the whole way."* **[PASS-1] Lan's farewell:** the first page of her ledger, first entry Seol's name dated nine years back — *"The ledger starts with you. Remember that when it feels like it started with losing."* — and "Take care of my little brother. He has never once listened to anyone."

**Gameplay identity:** Cage rows, patrols, the alarm wave — the mission Seol *leads*.

**Story purpose:** The largest liberation; Kang's judgment; the found-family's dispersal.

**Emotional purpose:** Seol's justice is restorative, not lethal; the family parts *as* family, alive, seeding a wider movement.

**Connection to ending:** Ohan's near-encounter sets the personal finale; the farewell means Seol goes to the ford with the movement already secured beyond her.

## MISSION 10 — "The Third Crossing" (FINALE)
**Scene IDs:** ch10 → a10_go → a10_wolf → [WAVE: Ohan boss] → a10_win → a10_return → a10_end → RESET
**Chapter code:** HOME

**Location:** The ford at Uiju — the exact place her father died. Dawn.

**Beat-by-beat:**
- **ch10:** The ford, "smaller than memory." Her village and her father's grave on the far bank; a column of freed people at her back. "Hooves on the west wind. The Wolf did not take the loss of his ledger kindly."
- **a10_wolf (Ohan) — [PASS-2 rewritten]:** He dismounts holding not a weapon but the Great Ledger. *"Baek Seol. Entry four hundred and six. I have read you. Two schools of the sword, a bow above her weight, and a habit of striking first… Batu died charging you. Batu was an animal. I am an inventory. I do not charge. I wait, and I collect. The property of the Khan does not walk away."* — *"Property doesn't. People do. And you can't read what we'll do next — that's the whole difference. Come count what it costs."*
- **[OHAN BOSS — the Wolf]:** Guard → three-strike flurry → spent window. He guards (attacks do 10%, no stagger), forces you to survive the flurry, then opens for double-damage punishment. The anti-Batu: patience and reading, not commitment.
- **a10_win:** *"Batu committed to lines. Ohan commits to nothing… So you give him the one thing no ledger holds: the turning form, improvised, two people moving as one shape that has never existed before this moment. The margin note was wrong — you don't fight like two schools. You fight like four people who loved you."* The Ledger goes into the black channel first; then the river takes Ohan.
- **a10_return (Seol) — [PASS-1, Danbi callback]:** The village comes out — and stops, the elder muttering "what must have happened to them out there." Then **Danbi** pushes through from the *village* side — word crossed the river faster than Seol — and says the words first: *"I am Danbi, daughter of this valley. I was taken.
