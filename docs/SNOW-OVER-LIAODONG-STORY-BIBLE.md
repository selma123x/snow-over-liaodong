# 1. COMPLETE STORY STRUCTURE

## Overview

Ten missions across three acts, fully linear (no branching), told in short scenes, floating subtitles, and five staged cutscenes. The player is **Baek Seol (백설, "white snow")**, a Korean girl taken from Joseon in the 1610s–1620s Ming–Qing transition, raised in a Chinese general's household, who becomes a liberator of captives and finally walks home.

**Act One — six missions:** her taking, her second family, her love, her first great victory.
**Act Two — two missions:** the collapse of Ming Liaodong and the theft of the network's secrets.
**Act Three — two missions:** the raid on the conquest's heart and the homecoming.

---

## PROLOGUE / MISSION 1 — "The Archer's Daughter"
- **Location:** Uiju, Joseon — a border village above the frozen Yalu. Winter. Seol is 11.
- **Purpose:** Establish who Seol is, who made her, and take both away in fifteen minutes.
- **Gameplay purpose:** Tutorial. Movement, the bow (X while untrained), fists, first wave fight against raiders.
- **Story purpose:** Plant the game's first and most important line — *"A blade in front of you is a problem. A blade behind you is a funeral. Never stop listening behind you."* — which structures the father's death, Jin's waist-grab save years later, and the final epilogue.
- **Important events:** Dawn archery drill → Jurchen raid (smoke, horns, riders in the millet) → Seol roped to a saddle → **the ford cutscene** (staged, 16s): her father catches seven raiders alone, kills six, embraces her — and the seventh man stabs him from behind while his arms are around her. *"He was holding you when he stopped listening."*
- **Character development:** Seol's competence and grief are both inherited. Her father's love is expressed as training.
- **Skills:** Bow (X, untrained basic) and fists — her father's gifts, present from the first minute.

## MISSION 2 — "The Market of Old Kang"
- **Location:** Guangning, Ming Liaodong. A slave market between the silk and salt-fish stalls. Weeks later.
- **Purpose:** Introduce the two men who will define her captivity and her rescue.
- **Gameplay purpose:** First real combat sandbox — fight Kang's handlers with bow/fists, free three cages (quick-gates).
- **Story purpose:** Establish Seol's defining reflex: given one free moment, she runs *toward the cages, not the gate*. Establish Kang (trafficker with a merchant's mask) and General Zhao (the man who sees a blade in a scrap heap).
- **Important events:** The careless knot → cage-freeing rampage → Kang backhands her to the stones ("Do you know what you have *cost* me?") → Zhao intervenes and buys her: *"I watched her fight. You had six men. She was winning."*
- **Character development:** Seol at her most feral; Zhao's respect precedes his affection.
- **Skills:** None new. Untrained kit (bow ②, fists X, no throw yet — Lan's ③ is locked until Year Three).

## MISSION 3 — "Five Winters" (The House of Zhao)
- **Location:** The Zhao garrison estate, Liaodong. Ages 11→16, one gate per year.
- **Purpose:** The heart of the game. Build the family the war will break.
- **Gameplay purpose:** Estate exploration; training dummies; the sword unlock mid-mission changes the combat kit permanently.
- **Story purpose:** Each year is one relationship: Y1 Mingzhu (belonging), Y2 Zhao (discipline), Y3 Lan and Jin (balance and its opposite), Y4 the Kang revelation (the plot engine), Y5 the dance/kiss/collapse (everything at once).
- **Important events:**
  - **Year One:** Mingzhu's brush and her defense at dinner ("In this house we name people or we starve"); her offhand line about the cave — *"I love that cave. For the freedom it allows."* — the seed for Mission 5. Seol thanks her in Chinese; "her whole face changes."
  - **Year Two:** The wooden sword by the sleeping mat. **Sword + trained kit unlock.**
  - **Year Three:** Lan's counter-throw ("Strength borrows. Balance *owns*.") — **skill ③ unlocks.** Jin introduced: the son who "dances," whom Seol spars to humiliate and loses to. *"It's just listening — with your whole body."*
  - **Year Four:** Zhao asks about her family in rough Korean; she names Kang as the raiders' master and an intelligence-seller to the Jurchen. Zhao begins his second ledger.
  - **Year Five:** **The kiss cutscene** (staged, 12s — camera drifts to the lanterns at contact) → **the Mingzhu death cutscene** (staged, 18s — camera descends for 14 seconds, then holds perfectly still while the snow falls) → the funeral aftermath (Zhao holding a wall up with his back; Lan's shaking hands; Seol: "you had a mother again, and you knew it only long enough to lose her"; the planted phrase *"guided by someone who knew the roads"*) → **the window** (Jin leaves without goodbye; finds her window without searching; three heartbeats) → **the vow**: Zhao's question — serve China or go home? — and her answer: *"You are my people too… but when we find Koreans in chains, we free them. Every one. That is my price."* The hug in which she feels her father's arms again.
- **Character development:** Seol gains and loses a second mother; Jin is established and removed; Zhao becomes a father through grief.
- **Skills:** Sword (trained kit, Y2, Zhao), skill ① dash (with training), skill ③ counter-throw (Y3, Lan).

## MISSION 4 — "Two Masks"
- **Location:** Rooftops of a fallen Liaodong town, deep night. Years later; Seol is an adult agent of Zhao's network.
- **Purpose:** The reunion — recognition through movement, not faces.
- **Gameplay purpose:** Full trained combat; patrol guards; the trap wave; **the Dance ult (④) unlocks at the recognition.**
- **Story purpose:** Pay off five years of the dance in one fight. Establish the adult relationship's wound (he left) and its terms (equals or nothing).
- **Important events:** Two masked crews clash over the same rescue → grudging split of the compound → empty cages, the trap springs → **the recognition cutscene** (staged, 10s — an accelerating camera orbit, "you know this dance, you know this *dancer*") → *"You left us. You left me."* → the waist-grab save (the father's death inverted: an embrace, danger from behind, but this time the blade doesn't land) → **"I had it."** and *"don't touch me, I'm mad at you"* → his answer: "I know you don't need me to save you. You never did." → the clumsy Korean riddle: *"Where the water is quiet like a mirror… where the mountain sees itself. When the sun is highest. Tomorrow."*
- **Character development:** Seol's pride and wound in one beat; Jin's first act of respect (he doesn't follow).
- **Skills:** ④ The Dance (ult) — unlocked at the recognition scene.

## MISSION 5 — "The Lake & The Cave"
- **Location:** A mountain lake at noon; then Jin's secret cave above the valley.
- **Purpose:** The quiet center of the game. Romance, forgiveness, Mingzhu's secret, the enchanted weapons.
- **Gameplay purpose:** The most varied mission: comic busy-work gates → traversal → the lake cutscene → **cave phase 1** (find five hidden wall-fragments, verticality, glowing gates) → **cave phase 2** (weapon hunt: the high ledge, the water's secret). The Winter Bow is a real upgrade (bow damage 15→22).
- **Story purpose:** Mingzhu becomes the story's secret heart; the romance is repaired *by the dead mother's letter*, not by an apology.
- **Important events:** The avoidance montage ("He left ME." / "A riddle. A RIDDLE. Like I'm twelve.") → the stand-by message → "…Aaaargh" → **the lake cutscene** (staged, 12s slow push across the mirror-water) and the golden-hour talk ("Neither of you apologizes exactly. Both of you forgive approximately.") → the cave: Jin's hidden life (echo → singing → drawing since age 8; "No one ever knew." / "Mingzhu did.") → the five fragments → the singing stone → **the lacquered box**: her paintings under her maiden-name seal; **three letters** — Jin's (predicting his leaving, forgiving it, *"two rivers pretending they don't share a sea… be together; love loudly, for both of us"*) and Seol's (*"For the daughter winter brought me"* — her blacksmith father, two hidden works) → **the Crane's Wing** (Jin's absurd reverse-curved sword, etched *"Dance, do not brawl; end, do not hate"*; flirtation: "It's hideous." / "It's *perfect*.") → **the Winter Bow** (etched in Korean she learned just for Seol: *"Snow may fall; the arrow flies home"*; Seol cries, then laughs, "and the acoustics of the cave forgive everything") → the intel: Kang and Batu move a column across the Yalu tonight. *"As equals, or not at all." / "As equals. Try to keep up."*
- **Character development:** Full repair of the romance; Mingzhu revealed as the household's hidden artist and its truest seer; Seol receives a mother's inheritance.
- **Skills:** The Winter Bow (permanent damage upgrade + weapon readout change).

## MISSION 6 — "The Third Water"
- **Location:** The frozen Yalu at night — the border itself, home visible on the far bank.
- **Purpose:** Act One's climax: the seventh man, the first great liberation, and the choice to turn *away* from home.
- **Gameplay purpose:** Strike chains (quick-gates), fight escort riders, then the **Batu boss wave**.
- **Story purpose:** Close the father's death; open the theme (what returning costs) via Danbi; end Act One on renunciation.
- **Important events:** Kang's column on the ice → **Batu**: *"The archer's girl. He was good, your father. Six of mine. I still count it." / "Count this."* → the duel — "Batu commits to a line, the way linear men do. You are never on it." — the ice takes him → Kang captured, ledgers burning → the crossing: **Danbi** (the girl afraid of what her family will say; Seol's order: *"Say it first, and say it loud. You survived. That is the whole story."*; Danbi gives her name and village — "the mill village below Uiju" — **this naming is in the build**) → the ending on the ice: *"There are more cages."* She turns from home "because turning from it is how you'll earn it." **End of Act One.**
- **Character development:** Seol avenges without being consumed; the thesis line is spoken for the first time.
- **Skills:** None new — Act One ends with the complete kit.

## MISSION 7 — "The City That Held a Country" (Act Two)
- **Location:** Liaoyang, the Ming eastern capital, burning. Night. (Historical: the 1621 fall, including the real detail of traitors opening the sluice gates.)
- **Purpose:** The war arrives at full scale; save Zhao; reconcile father and son.
- **Gameplay purpose:** Burning-city combat; wave fight to reach the keep.
- **Story purpose:** Zhao's refusal of the historical defector's path; the game's best father-son beat.
- **Important events:** **Lan at the barricade** — her counter-ledger speech (*"Kang counts what he takes. I count what we take back… mine will be longer than his"*) and her mother's letter ("I'm starting with a barricade") → the keep: Zhao writing "the last dispatch no one will ever read" — *"Li Yongfang knelt at Fushun and they made him rich. I would rather be poor and my own"* → Seol: "A man should *live* where he's loved… choose the war you can still win." / "…Mingzhu used to argue like that. Carry me out of my grave." → **the rooftop road**: Zhao watches Jin's dancers evacuate civilians — *"I called it dancing. All those years. I said it like a small word." / "It is dancing." / "It is the biggest word I know now."*
- **Character development:** Zhao completes his arc of seeing; Lan becomes a person (PASS 1).
- **Skills:** None; full kit.

## MISSION 8 — "The Ledger Road"
- **Location:** A mountain pass in the western hills, midnight. Weeks later.
- **Purpose:** Kang's escape and betrayal; introduce the Wolf; Jin's growth scene.
- **Gameplay purpose:** Terraced night traversal; convoy ambush wave.
- **Story purpose:** Stakes for Act Three (the Great Ledger — every route, safehouse, and name — sold to the conquerors); the romance's adult milestone.
- **Important events:** Kang escaped in Liaoyang's chaos and sold the Great Ledger to **Ohan of the Plain Blue Banner** ("the Wolf") → the convoy: *"Box or people first?" / "People. Always people."* → the letter of sale (Ohan named; the original travels with Kang to Shenyang) → **"Two Rivers" (PASS 1)**: Jin's night-watch confession — *"All my life I've moved around things… There's no around, where we're going. There's only through. That's your art, not mine."* — and his request that **she lead the Shenyang operation** ("You cut lines. I draw circles. Shenyang needs a line."), the structural inverse of the waist-grab; then the phrasebook Korean, practiced for her village: "I should greet them properly." *"You do not tell him his accent is terrible. You memorize it instead."*
- **Character development:** Jin's arc completes in words (deference as love); Ohan is seeded by name and title one mission before his appearance.
- **Skills:** None; full kit.

## MISSION 9 — "The Belly of the Conquest" (Act Three)
- **Location:** The holding yards of Shenyang, the conquest's capital. Night.
- **Purpose:** The largest liberation; Kang's judgment; the farewell to Zhao and Lan.
- **Gameplay purpose:** Cage rows, patrols, the alarm wave — the mission Seol *leads*.
- **Story purpose:** The game's moral thesis applied to its villain: judgment belongs to the wronged.
- **Important events:** Three cage-row rescues (an old man's granddaughter; children going over the wall in the dancers' ribbons, "laughing — laughing — at the ride") → **the counting house**: Kang doesn't run — "Name a price. Everyone has one. I have met *everyone*." / *"You met me twice. The day you sold me, and tonight. Both times you counted wrong."* → the freed fill the doorway → *"I'm not going to kill you… They get to. You're going home with them — roped to the last cart, past every village you emptied. Let them do the counting."* → **Two Roads**: Zhao west with Lan, Seol east with the freed — *"You were never my servant. You were my second daughter from the day I watched you fight six men with your hands tied wrong." / "They were tied fine. I loosened them myself." / "I know. Go home, daughter. Walk tall the whole way."* → **Lan's farewell (PASS 1)**: the first page of her ledger, first entry Seol's name, nine years old — *"The ledger starts with you. Remember that when it feels like it started with losing."* — and "Take care of my little brother. He has never once listened to anyone."
- **Character development:** Seol's justice is restorative, not lethal; the found family parts as family.
- **Skills:** None; full kit.

## MISSION 10 — "The Third Crossing" (Finale)
- **Location:** The ford at Uiju — the exact place her father died. Dawn.
- **Purpose:** Full circle: the last battle where the first one was lost; the homecoming; the grave.
- **Gameplay purpose:** Final boss — Ohan + four riders (boss 210 HP; **currently a stat-boss, no unique mechanic** — flagged in the director's review, scheduled for PASS 2).
- **Story purpose:** The hwanhyangnyeo confrontation; the thesis said at home; the epilogue closes the first lesson.
- **Important events:** **Ohan**: *"The property of the Khan does not walk away." / "Property doesn't. People do. Come count what the difference costs."* → the fight — "this time the dance is beside you… Ohan commits to his line. You were never on it." → **the homecoming** (⚠ **as built**: Seol's original monologue — *"They walked home. That is the whole story. Anyone who thinks otherwise can try surviving it first."* — the mother's hand closing the inch, the grandmother's homecoming song. **The Danbi callback and Jin's phrasebook bow are NOT in this scene in the current build** — the PASS-1 edit failed silently) → **the epilogue**: the Winter Bow's first arrow laid on her father's grave; Jin at the bank "at that respectful distance he has finally, *finally* learned"; shoulder to shoulder, "close enough that neither of you is behind the other"; *"A blade behind you is a funeral." / "Then it's good — that you will never fight alone again."* (in Korean, barely clumsy at all anymore). Snow over both countries. **끝.**
- **Character development:** Seol carries her teachers home; the first lesson is answered by love.
- **Skills:** None; the ending is played with everything four people gave her.

# 2. CHARACTER BIBLE

## BAEK SEOL (백설, "White Snow") — protagonist
- **Background:** Only child of a widowed archer of the Uiju border garrison, Joseon. Taken by Jurchen raiders at 11 after watching her father die mid-embrace; sold in Guangning; bought at 11 by General Zhao after freeing three cages during her own escape attempt; raised in his household to 16; then an agent-liberator of his covert network into adulthood.
- **Motivations:** Open cages. Initially rage-shaped (avenging her taking), matured into vocation (Lan's ledger of the freed literally begins with her name).
- **Beliefs:** Judgment belongs to the wronged; shame belongs to the ones who ask the wrong first question; loyalty is chosen, not national — *"You are my people too."*
- **Relationship with Seol:** —
- **Arc:** Feral orphan → disciplined student → wounded lover → commander → the woman who can turn away from home in order to earn it → the woman who brings everyone home, including herself.
- **Biggest emotional moments:** Feeling her father's arms go slack; thanking Mingzhu in Chinese; the vow-hug where two fathers' arms overlap; "I had it… don't touch me, I'm mad at you"; crying-then-laughing at the Korean etching on the Winter Bow; "Walk tall the whole way"; the arrow on the grave.
- **Voice rule:** Terse. Deflects with dryness ("I had nothing better to do"). Her longest speeches are orders given to protect other people's dignity. (Noted in the director's review: the Kang confrontation is her longest speech and slightly over her register.)

## JIN (진) — deuteragonist, love interest
- **Background:** Zhao's son, Lan's younger brother, Mingzhu's dove. A dancer in a soldier's house; secretly an artist since childhood (the cave: echo → singing → drawing at 8, "no one ever knew"). Left home after his mother's death; returned years later as the masked leader of a troupe of dancer-rescuers (ribbons, hooks, misdirection — no one touched).
- **Motivations:** To move *around* what hurts (his own admission) — grief, his father, walls — until Shenyang, where he chooses *through* by following her.
- **Beliefs:** "Dance, do not brawl; end, do not hate" (his grandfather's etching, which he lives before he reads it). Listening with the whole body as ethics, not just technique.
- **Relationship with Seol:** Rival-classmate → teacher → first kiss → the one who left → masked stranger → recognized partner → equal ("As equals, or not at all") → the man who asks her to lead → shoulder to shoulder at the ford.
- **Arc:** Dismissed son → runaway → master of his own art → the man who learns that respect is the truest form of love: he stops saving her and starts *trusting* her. His growth is measured in three physical acts: the waist-grab (instinct), not following her (restraint), and standing beside her "close enough that neither of you is behind the other" (arrival).
- **Biggest emotional moments:** The window (three heartbeats, no goodbye); "I know you don't need me to save you — you never did"; the Korean riddle; reading his mother's letter "wrecked and lit at once"; "Shenyang frightens me"; the last line of the game.
- **Voice rule:** Even, warm, a half-step more playful than the scene; speaks Korean clumsily and on purpose.

## GENERAL ZHAO (趙) — the second father
- **Background:** Ming garrison commander in Liaodong; eleven years holding his line; a man whose vocabulary of love is drills, nods, and ledgers.
- **Motivations:** Duty first, then — after Mingzhu — the preservation of the people duty was supposed to protect. He refuses Li Yongfang's path (defection-for-riches) explicitly.
- **Beliefs:** "A man should die where he stood" → corrected by Seol to "live where he's loved." Merit over origin (he buys a Korean slave because she was *winning*).
- **Relationship with Seol:** Purchaser → drillmaster → the man who asks her the question → father in everything but blood → "You were never my servant. You were my second daughter."
- **Arc:** The blind spot arc: instantly reads Kang's treason and Seol's worth, cannot see his own son — until the rooftop road, where "dancing" becomes "the biggest word I know now."
- **Biggest emotional moments:** "You had six men. She was winning"; the wooden sword by the mat; the vow-hug; "Mingzhu used to argue like that… carry me out of my grave"; the rooftop; "Walk tall the whole way."

## LAN (蘭) — the sister
- **Background:** Zhao and Mingzhu's daughter, four years Seol's senior; drilled Seol in the counter-throw; adult role: logistics and intelligence chief of the network ("the sister-network's cipher" is hers).
- **Motivations (PASS 1):** The counter-ledger — her mother taught her sums; she keeps books of the *freed*, determined that her ledger outgrow Kang's.
- **Beliefs:** "Strength borrows. Balance *owns*." Forgiveness as labor: "Her letter said I should forgive the world for being what it is. I'm starting with a barricade."
- **Relationship with Seol:** Drill-sister → "Little sister. You did grow up." → the woman whose ledger begins with Seol's name.
- **Arc:** Skill-teacher → barricade commander → the keeper of the story's accounting; entrusts Jin to Seol ("He has never once listened to anyone").
- **Biggest emotional moments:** Shaking hands over her mother's accounts at the funeral; the barricade speech; the folded first page; the hug of a woman who doesn't do hugs.

## LADY MINGZHU (明珠, "Bright Pearl") — the secret heart
- **Background:** Zhao's wife; daughter of a master blacksmith "the finest in three provinces, though the world forgot, because his daughter was only permitted to marry, not to forge." Secretly an artist her whole married life — paintings signed with her maiden name, made in a cave where no one could see, having discovered her son's refuge when he was eight and said nothing, "because a named freedom dies."
- **Motivations:** To give her children freedom she was never permitted — invisibly, so it would survive.
- **Beliefs:** "In this house we name people or we starve." Freedom as expression; love as foresight (she predicted Jin's flight and forgave it in advance; she saw the romance "by the pond — two rivers pretending they don't share a sea" — and blessed it in writing).
- **Relationship with Seol:** Teacher → defender → "your mother in everything but blood" → poser of the game's central image (the Winter Bow, etched in Korean *she learned for Seol*: "Snow may fall; the arrow flies home").
- **Arc:** The posthumous arc — alive, she is the household's quiet order; dead, she is revealed (fragments → letters → weapons) as the person who saw everyone truly and armed them across time. The best-developed character in the game precisely because the cave restructures every earlier scene she touched.
- **Biggest emotional moments:** Her face changing at Seol's first Chinese; the cave line at eleven; the death cutscene's final stillness; every letter.
- **Death:** Killed in Year Five in a strike on the eastern estate — "fast riders, guided by someone who knew the roads" (planted as Kang's doing; the phrase recurs).

## KANG (康) — the merchant of everything
- **Background:** Guangning "merchant" whose real trade is the machinery of taking: he *sends* the raiders (Batu reports to him), sells the captives, and sells border intelligence north to the Jurchen. Later author of the Great Ledger; escapes Liaoyang's fall; sells the network to Ohan; ends in his Shenyang counting house.
- **Motivations:** Price. Nothing else has ever been real to him.
- **Beliefs:** "Everyone has one. I have met *everyone*." — a creed the ending refutes to his face.
- **Relationship with Seol:** Her seller; her first backhand; her mirror-opposite (he counts the taken; she and Lan count the freed).
- **Arc:** Untouchable middleman → exposed traitor → fugitive salesman → judged, not by her sword but by his merchandise: roped to the last cart, marched past every village he emptied, "let them do the counting."
- **Biggest emotional moments:** "Do you know what you have *cost* me?"; the counting-house scene, where his creed fails on the one person he met twice.

## BATU — the seventh man
- **Background:** Jurchen raider captain in Kang's employ; the man who stabbed Seol's father from behind mid-embrace; years later, Kang's escort partner on the Yalu column.
- **Motivations:** A professional. His only sentiment is a tally — "Six of mine. I still count it."
- **Beliefs:** Force in a straight line; he "commits to a line, the way linear men do."
- **Relationship with Seol:** The wound itself. Her father's murderer; the reason she listens behind her.
- **Arc:** Off-page menace → named memory → the Act One boss, killed by everything she learned since him — the archer, the general, the dancer — and taken by her own river, "with a single black swallow." Dies at the *end of Act One*, not the game (noted in the director's review as the structural trade for Ohan).
- **Biggest emotional moment:** "He was good, your father." — the only respect he knows how to pay, and the last thing he says before "Count this."

## OHAN — the Wolf of the Plain Blue Banner (final boss)
- **Background:** A Banner officer of the rising Jin/Qing state; buyer of Kang's Great Ledger ("the whole apparatus, for services rendered"); pursues the freed column to the Uiju ford.
- **Motivations:** Recovery of "the property of the Khan"; the bureaucratic face of the same machine Batu was the fist of.
- **Beliefs:** People as property — the exact belief the game exists to refute; Seol's reply is the theme in one line: "Property doesn't [walk away]. People do."
- **Relationship with Seol:** None personal — and the review flags this honestly: he is the least-developed antagonist fighting the most important battle. He is *seeded* one mission early (named in the letter of sale, titled "the Wolf") but has two spoken lines total. PASS 2 will address presence and mechanics.
- **Arc as built:** Named threat → ford duel → "the river takes what it is owed, and closes."

## DANBI — the seed that grows
- **Background:** A freed captive on the Yalu ice, Seol's own age, from "the mill village below Uiju."
- **In the current build:** She hesitates at the crossing, receives the thesis as an order ("Say it first, and say it loud"), squares her shoulders, gives her name "like a debt acknowledged," and walks. **Her planned homecoming return (saying the words first, before Seol) is written but NOT in the deployed build** — the edit failed silently; the homecoming still plays with Seol's monologue alone.

## Named historical/political figures (referenced, not appearing)
- **Li Yongfang** — the real Ming commander who surrendered Fushun (1618) and was enriched and absorbed by the Jurchen state; in-game, the path Zhao explicitly refuses, twice.
- **The Khan** — Nurhaci, referenced by title only ("the property of the Khan").
- **The Eight Banners / Plain Blue Banner** — the real Jurchen/Qing military-social system; Ohan's affiliation.

# 3. SKILL / STORY CONNECTION

The game's central design conceit: **Seol has no stats — she has teachers.** Every combat verb is a person.

| Slot | Skill | Giver | Why they give it | What it says about them | Where it pays off |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| **② LB** | The Bow | Her father | "Dawn after dawn, until the string sings for you" — the far fight | His whole inheritance, given gladly | Present in every mission; transfigured in Mission 5 into the Winter Bow; "the arrows fly home" in the final fight |
| **X (trained)** | The Sword | **Zhao** (Year Two) | He sees footwork in how she beats a quilt; the wooden sword by the mat is his entire declaration of belief | Merit-blindness to origin; love expressed as discipline; "praise is a nod" | The trained kit carries Acts Two and Three; "trained… by a general of Ming" in Batu's defeat |
| **① RB** | Swallow Step (dash-strike) | Zhao's training (with the sword) | A soldier's answer: close the distance, end it | Directness as doctrine | The gap-closer of every wave fight |
| **③ RT** | Counter-Throw | **Lan** (Year Three) | "Strength borrows. Balance *owns*." — taught by dumping Seol on her back nine times | Lan's whole philosophy: absorb the world's force and return it with the ground attached — later literalized as the counter-ledger | The heavy answer to every rush; thematically, the game's justice model (Kang's force returned to him by the people he moved) |

# 4. HISTORICAL / POLITICAL STRUCTURE

## Where the story sits
The **Ming–Later Jin (Qing) transition in Liaodong, c. 1618–1620s** — the decade in which Nurhaci's unified Jurchen state tore the Ming northeast apart:
- **Fushun, 1618** — fell largely by the defection of **Li Yongfang**, who was ennobled and married into the Aisin Gioro clan: the historical template for "kneel and be made rich," which Zhao names and refuses.
- **Liaoyang, 1621** — the Ming eastern capital, fallen with the real recorded detail the game uses: betrayal from within, **sluice gates opened**. Mission 7 is set during this fall.
- **Shenyang (Mukden)** — taken in the same 1621 campaign; the rising state's seat and, in-game, the site of the holding yards.
- **The Eight Banners** — the real military-social organization of the Jurchen/Qing state; Ohan belongs to the historical **Plain Blue Banner**.

## The three powers
- **Ming China** — the collapsing order. Represented by Zhao: the loyalist who will not defect but learns that the war worth winning is his people, not his post. The game's Ming is neither romanticized (its slave markets buy Korean children; its magistrates' wives sneer "that Korean thing") nor villainized — it is a house falling on everyone inside it.
- **Joseon Korea** — home, wound, and destination. Present at the start (Uiju, the garrison, the raid) and the end (the homecoming); in between it exists as language, songs, graves, and the people in the cages. The returned-women material — the stigma against **hwanhyangnyeo** — anchors the ending. *Scholarly caveat, documented honestly:* that discourse in its canonical form belongs to the post-1636 Qing invasions of Joseon; this story sits in the 1620s. The stigma against returned captives absolutely existed earlier, so the ending is defensible, but a historian would call the usage slightly anticipatory.
- **The Jurchen / Later Jin** — the ascending power. Deliberately not a monolith of evil: the game's true villain is **the machinery of taking** — and its architect, Kang, is a Ming subject. Batu and Ohan are the machine's fist and its bureaucracy; the conquest is context, not the crime.

## Political conflicts as the story uses them
1. **Loyalty vs. survival** (the Li Yongfang question) — Zhao's arc.
2. **Nation vs. person** — Seol's answer to "China or Korea?" is neither: "You are my people too… we free them. Every one." A war orphan builds a third allegiance out of two states that failed her.
3. **Commerce inside catastrophe** — Kang: war as a market, intelligence and human beings as fungible goods; the Great Ledger as the era's true weapon.
4. **The price of return** — the hwanhyangnyeo theme: the war's cruelty outlives the war, administered at home by shame.

## How fiction meets record
All named protagonists are fictional; all load-bearing events, institutions, and the one named historical figure (Li Yongfang) are real. The fictional layer (a female fighter-liberator, dancer-rescuers, two heirloom "enchanted" weapons — enchanted by meaning, not magic) is genre and never contradicts the record; it moves through the gaps the record leaves.

# 5. FULL DIALOGUE / STORY SCENES (as built)

### The father's lessons and death (Mission 1)
**His Lessons:** "Again," he says, and you draw the bow again. Then empty hands, hip and shoulder, the throw that ends arguments. He gives you both, gladly — the far fight and the near one. "A blade in front of you is a problem," he says. "A blade behind you is a funeral. *Never* stop listening behind you."

**The Ford (cutscene, 16s):** *He catches them at the ford. One man, on foot, against seven. / You watch your father take them apart — the first with an arrow, the last with his hands. / Then his arms are around you, and he is saying your name into your hair — / — and his arms go slack. You feel it before you understand it. / A blade behind you is a funeral. He was holding you when he stopped listening.*

**He Comes For You (scene):** …Six men down in the snow. Then his arms are around you, and he is saying your name into your hair, and for one breath the world is safe — and his arms go slack. You feel it before you understand it. The seventh man. From behind.

### First meeting — Kang and Zhao (Mission 2)
**Old Kang:** The crowd parts and the fat merchant is there, breathing hard, rings on every finger. He looks at his emptied cages, then at you, almost with wonder. "Do you know," he says pleasantly, "what you have *cost* me?" — and the back of his hand puts you on the stones.

**The General:** A shadow between you and the second blow. …"Then you won't mind selling it cheap." He does not look at Kang. He is looking at you the way a man looks at a blade he has found in a scrap heap. "I watched her fight," he says. "You had six men. She was winning."

### First meetings — Mingzhu, Zhao's yard, Lan and Jin (Mission 3)
**Lady Mingzhu (Year One):** The general's wife does not coo at you. She puts a brush in your hand. "Characters first. Then manners. Then everything else." … "In this house," she says, "we name people or we starve. Which will you choose?" … She nods at a dark seam in the rock above the lake valley. "I love that cave," she says, to no one in particular. "For the freedom it allows." You are eleven. You file it under things grown women say. — That night, unprompted, you thank her — in Chinese. Her whole face changes.

**The General's Yard (Year Two):** Zhao watches you beat the dust out of a quilt and sees the footwork in it. The next dawn there is a wooden sword by your sleeping mat. Your father gave you a soldier's knife; the general means to give you a swordsman's forms. — He drills you like a soldier, not a ward. Praise is a nod. You would kill for the nod.

**Lan and Jin (Year Three):** Lan… drills you the way winter drills a mountain. "Strength borrows," she says, dumping you on your back for the ninth time. "Balance *owns*." — And Jin — her little brother, your age, ink on his fingers, forever humming… the general walks away in disgust. "My son dances." — You spar Jin once, to put the slacker in his place. You lose. He was never where your blade was — like fencing smoke that smiles. "It's just listening," he says, helping you up, infuriatingly kind. "With your whole body."

### The Kang revelation (Year Four)
One evening Zhao asks about your family — in Korean. Rough, careful Korean… So you tell him: the raid, the river, and the thing he does not know — *the raiders reported to Kang.* … "I thought he traded slaves," he says at last. "He trades in my country's throat." After that, the general starts keeping a second ledger, in his own hand, of everything Kang moves.

### The kiss (cutscene + scene, Year Five)
**Cutscene (12s):** *"Stop trying to beat me. Move with me." / And one evening you stop fighting it — / — and the two of you are one turning shape in the lantern light, blade-edge to blade-edge, never touching. / The form ends. Neither of you steps back. / The distance closes by itself.* (Camera circles the pond, drifting upward to the lanterns at the moment of contact.)

**Scene:** …The kiss tastes like the beginning of something. It is actually the end of everything.

### Mingzhu's death (cutscene + aftermath, Year Five)
**Cutscene (18s):** *Fushun falls. Then Tieling. The names come like ice cracking on a warming river. / It reaches the house on a market morning. A strike on the eastern estate — fast riders, guided by someone who knew the roads. / Lady Mingzhu was overseeing the granary there. / They bring her home in the cart she left in. / The general does not weep. Lan does not weep. Jin goes still — and stays still. / China is falling. In this house, it began with her.* (Camera descends for 14 seconds, then holds perfectly still for the last four while snow falls.)

**The North Falls (aftermath):** The funeral is white, and enormous, and wrong — she would have hated the expense. Zhao stands through all of it like a man holding a wall up with his back. Lan runs the household's grief the way she runs everything: lists, provisions, guests fed — and only you see her hands shake over the accounts her mother taught her. And you — you keep reaching for the anger you learned at eleven… It doesn't come. What comes instead is quieter and worse: you had a mother again, and you knew it only long enough to lose her. — *Guided by someone who knew the roads. You will remember that phrase. Later, you will understand it.*

### The window and the vow (Year Five)
**The Window:** Jin grieves the way he does everything — in motion arrested. Days of stillness, the dancer gone stone. … At the gate he looks up. Finds your window without searching, as if he always knew which one it was. Holds your eyes for three heartbeats. — Then he is gone. He never said goodbye to you. Perhaps that was the goodbye.

**The Question:** "When this war comes for us — and it is coming — will you serve China? Or will you go home, to your people?" — "**You are my people too.** I'll fight with you. But when we find Koreans in chains on this road — and we will — we free them. Every one. That is my price." — The general… crosses the room and holds you like a daughter. And in his arms you feel other arms, at a river ford, five years gone, and the tears come without asking.

### The recognition sequence (Mission 4)
**Cutscene (10s):** *There are too many. Thinking stops. Your body chooses for you — / — and it chooses the pond, the lanterns, the turning form — / — and the stranger flows into it with you, like he has been waiting years for the invitation. / You know this dance. You know this dancer.* (Accelerating camera orbit.)

**The Shape of You:** …It ends. Everyone else is down. Two masks in the torch smoke, breathing hard. "…**Jin?**"

**You Left:** "You left us." A breath. "You left *me*." — He doesn't defend himself. His eyes… move past your shoulder and go flat, and he *dashes* — hand at your waist, pulling you hard against him, his blade going out over your shoulder in the same motion — into the throat of a soldier who had risen behind you. — For half a heartbeat you are sixteen, by a pond, in lantern light.

**I Had It:** "**I had it.**" Your voice is level and dangerous. Then, a half-second later, entirely unlevel: "And *don't touch me*. I'm mad at you." — He raises both hands, steps back — and doesn't argue. "I know you don't need me to save you," he says quietly. "You never did." — "I need to think. Don't follow me." — He bows his head — respect, not surrender. Then, at the roof's edge… In Korean. Clumsy, careful, learned-word-by-word Korean: "물이 거울처럼 조용한 곳… 산이 자기를 보는 곳. 해가 제일 높을 때. 내일." — *He speaks Korean. When did he — he's gone.*

### The lake (Mission 5)
**Cutscene (12s):** *The water is glass. The mountain stands upside down in it. / He is there at the shore — and for once, not moving at all. / As if stillness were the gift he prepared. / "You came," he says. In Korean. Still clumsy. Still learned for you.*

**Scene:** "I had nothing better to do," you say, in Chinese, and sit down beside him anyway. You talk until the mirror turns gold… Neither of you apologizes exactly. Both of you forgive approximately. Then he stands, offers his hand, and doesn't quite meet your eyes. "There's somewhere I want to show you."

### The cave sequence (Mission 5) — full chain
**His Whole Hidden Life:** …lanterns, hanging silk paintings, calligraphy drying on lines, a spring pooling black and gold… "When did you start coming here?" — "Small. With friends first — for the echo… Then alone, to sing. The acoustics forgive everything. Then, when I was eight — to draw. Away from them. No one ever knew." — "Mingzhu did," you say, before you think. "She told me once she loved this cave. *For the freedom it allowed — the freedom to express what she couldn't say.*" — Jin does not move. Jin does not breathe. "…She *what?*"

**Written Where No One Reads (the five fragments):** *"I followed my son when he was eight, and found his kingdom, and said nothing — because a named freedom dies. So I came alone instead, when the house grew too small for everything I was not permitted to say. I wrote in the dark, where no one reads. It was the only freedom on offer, and it was enough, because from these walls I watched my children become themselves. If you are reading this, you learned to see in the dark, as I did. Look beneath the singing stone."* — Jin's voice, when it finally comes, is a boy's: "The singing stone. Where the echo is best. I stood on it a thousand times."

**The Lacquered Box (Jin's letter):** *"You will leave, my dove. Grief will make you run, because your heart moves before your feet, it always has. Do not be ashamed of it. But go back for the girl. I watched you two by the pond — two rivers pretending they don't share a sea. What I could not say aloud, I say here: be together. Life is short; the ice is long; love loudly, for both of us."* — He looks up at you, wrecked and lit at once. — She predicted the window. She forgave him years before he did it. And so — finally, fully, out loud in your own chest — so do you. → **"We're alright, Jin. We were always going to be."**

**The Third Letter:** *"For the daughter winter brought me. Seol. My father was a master blacksmith — the finest in three provinces, though the world forgot, because his daughter was only permitted to marry, not to forge. Two of his works I kept. I leave them hidden in this cave, one for each of you, where only people who see in the dark deserve to find them. Take the high ledge and the water's secret. The steel remembers what I could not say. — Your mother in everything but blood."*

**The Crane's Wing:** …a sword like nothing sane — blade curved the wrong way, weighted at the tip… A *terrible* sword. For anyone on earth but one person. …Etched down the spine: 舞而不斗, 斷而不怨 — *"Dance, do not brawl; end, do not hate."* — "It's hideous," you say, honestly, a little jealous of neither the sword nor the man, obviously, shut up. — "It's *perfect*," he breathes, and spins it again, showing off now, absolutely showing off for you.

**The Winter Bow:** …a bow of black horn and pale birch, strung with something that sings when the air touches it. It draws like breathing out… Etched along the upper limb: 눈은 내리되, 활은 집으로 — *"Snow may fall; the arrow flies home."* — "She etched yours in Korean," Jin says quietly. "She must have learned the letters just to —" He stops, because you are crying, and then you are laughing, and the acoustics of the cave forgive everything.

**Kang (the intel):** "…Tonight he moves a whole column of Koreans across the ice. His Jurchen partner rides escort. A raider captain called **Batu**." — The name goes through you like river cold. — "My people are already moving," Jin says. He looks at you — beside him, this time, not in front. "Fight with us. With *me*. As equals, or not at all." — "As equals," you say. "Try to keep up."

### Batu (Mission 6)
"The archer's girl," Batu says at last, and smiles. "He was good, your father. Six of mine. I still count it." — "Count this," you say, and draw. → **Settled:** He is strong. You are *trained* — by an archer of Joseon, by a general of Ming, by a dancer who taught you to listen with your whole body. Batu commits to a line, the way linear men do. You are never on it. The ice takes him with a single black swallow, and the river — your river — closes over the seventh man.

### Danbi on the ice (Mission 6 — in the build)
"The far bank is Joseon… Walk. Don't run — the ice holds if you walk." — A girl your old age hesitates. "My family — what they'll say, about where I've been—" — "Then say it first, and say it loud… You survived. That is the whole story. Anyone ashamed of you should try surviving it themselves." You say it like an order, because she needs it to be one. She squares her shoulders. — "Danbi," she says, at the edge of the ice — her name, offered like a debt acknowledged. "From the mill village below Uiju. If you ever cross—" She doesn't finish it. She walks.

### End of Act One
"We can come back across whenever you want," he says. In Korean. Getting better at it. — "I know," you say. "First — we go back to work. There are more cages." You look once more at the woodsmoke of home, and turn from it, because turning from it is how you'll earn it.

### Act Two — Liaoyang (Mission 7)
**Lan:** "He won't come… Li Yongfang knelt at Fushun and they made him rich. Father says he would rather be poor and *his own*." — "Then we don't ask. We carry." — "Little sister. You did grow up. …Mother taught me sums, so I keep books. Kang counts what he takes. I count what we take *back* — every name we free goes in my ledger. When this is over, mine will be longer than his. …Her letter said I should forgive the world for being what it is. I'm starting with a barricade."

**Zhao:** "I held this line eleven years. A man should die where he stood." — "A man should *live* where he's loved… This position is lost. Your people are not. Choose the war you can still win." — "…Mingzhu used to argue like that. Very well, daughter. Carry me out of my grave."

**The rooftop road:** Zhao watches his son lift an old woman across a gap as gently as a bow across strings. "I called it dancing. All those years. I said it like a small word." — "It is dancing." — "Yes. It is the biggest word I know now."

### Act Two — the pass (Mission 8)
**The convoy:** "Box or people first?" — "People. Always people. Then we burn his paperwork." → **The letter of sale:** *"To Ohan of the Plain Blue Banner — the whole apparatus, for services rendered. The original follows with me to Shenyang. — K."*

**Two Rivers (Jin's night watch):** "Shenyang frightens me. …All my life I've moved *around* things. Grief. My father. Walls. It's what the dance is — the art of never being where the blow lands. There's no around, where we're going. There's only through. That's your art, not mine. So I'm asking. Lead the yards. I'll put my dancers wherever you point. You cut lines. I draw circles. Shenyang needs a line." — It costs him something to hand you that, and you both know it, and he hands it over anyway. — Later, half-asleep against your shoulder: 안녕하십니까. 저는… 진입니다. "If I'm going to meet your village, I should greet them properly." You do not tell him his accent is terrible. You memorize it instead.

### Act Three — Kang's judgment (Mission 9)
"Name a price. Everyone has one. I have met *everyone*." — "You met me twice. The day you sold me, and tonight. Both times you counted wrong." — Behind you, the yards stand open, and the people he priced fill the doorway — hundreds of them, silent, looking at him. — "I'm not going to kill you. I'm not even going to judge you. *They* get to. You're going home with them — to Joseon, roped to the last cart, past every village you emptied. Let them do the counting."

### The farewell (Mission 9)
**Zhao:** "You said once I was your people too. Hear the other half. You were never my servant, Seol. You were my second daughter from the day I watched you fight six men with your hands tied wrong." — "They were tied fine. I loosened them myself." — "I know. Go home, daughter. Walk tall the whole way."
**Lan:** a folded page — the first sheet of her ledger; the first entry, in her precise hand, your name and a date nine years old. "You were the first one we took back. The ledger starts with you. Remember that when it feels like it started with losing." … "Take care of my little brother. He listens to you. He has never once listened to anyone."

### The finale (Mission 10)
**Ohan:** "The property of the Khan does not walk away." — "Property doesn't. People do. Come count what the difference costs." — *On this ford your father fought seven men. You are not your father. You are what he made, finished by three more hands. And you are not alone.* → **What the River Keeps:** It ends where it began: on river ice, against wolf-fur — but this time the dance is beside you… the arrows fly home the way a mother promised they would. Ohan commits to his line. You were never on it. The river takes what it is owed, and closes.

**The homecoming (⚠ as built — no Danbi):** They come out to the road — and stop. …So you step into the road, and you say it loud enough for both banks of the river: "Every person behind me crossed hell to stand on this dirt. If your first question is what was *done to them* instead of what they *survived*, you are asking about your own shame, not theirs. They walked home. That is the whole story. Anyone who thinks otherwise can try surviving it first." — Silence. Then the mother's hand closes the inch. Then the whole road is arms and weeping, and the elder is very quiet, and somewhere a grandmother starts the song for homecomings that no one has needed in nine years.

**The epilogue:** Your father's grave looks north over the ford he held. You lay the Winter Bow's first arrow on the stone — the far kill and the near one, both come home… Jin waits at the bank, at that respectful distance he has finally, *finally* learned. …He just settles shoulder to shoulder, facing the water, close enough that neither of you is behind the other. — "A blade behind you is a funeral," you say. The old lesson. The first one. — "Then it's good," he says, in Korean — barely clumsy at all anymore — "that you will never fight alone again." — Snow begins over the river, over both countries, over the graves and the woodsmoke and the long roads west — falling, like you always will now, on everything it loves without having to choose. — **끝 — The End.**
